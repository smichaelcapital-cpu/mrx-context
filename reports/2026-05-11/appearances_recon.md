## SUMMARY

**Total blocks reviewed:** 6 (across 4 calendar years: 2022–2026)

### Variation axes observed

1. **Page count**: 1 page (wunstell, olsen) / 2 pages (williams, butler, black_bp) / 6 pages (halprin). Driven by number of parties. The 2-page BP MDL pattern is a specific artifact of that case's fixed defendant set.

2. **Role label format**: Three distinct styles observed:
   - `Attorney for PLAINTIFF:` / `Attorney for DEFENDANTS:` (BP MDL, mixed case)
   - `ATTORNEY FOR PLAINTIFF:` / `FOR THE DEFENDANTS, [full legal description]:` (halprin, all caps with verbose party identification)
   - `Attorney for Claimants:` / `Attorney for Respondents:` (olsen arbitration)
   - Role labels must be treated as opaque strings from the data file — not an enum.

3. **Videographer/ALSO PRESENT placement and format**:
   - Dedicated overflow page with `ALSO PRESENT:` header (williams, butler, black_bp)
   - Last appearances page with `ALSO PRESENT:` header (halprin page 10, wunstell page 4)
   - Inline labeled line `VIDEOGRAPHER: [Name]` without ALSO PRESENT header (olsen)
   - Three structurally different patterns; renderer needs to handle all three.

4. **NOT PRESENT annotation**: Present in halprin (6 firms) and all three BP MDL cases (2 firms each). Absent in wunstell and olsen. NOT PRESENT replaces the `BY:` line entirely — but the firm address and email are still listed.

5. **Zoom annotation**: "(Via Zoom)" / "(via Zoom)" inline after ESQ. (halprin, olsen). Absent in all 2022 BP cases. Capitalization is inconsistent even within the same case series (olsen 032025 vs 040825).

6. **Non-attorney in ALSO PRESENT**: wunstell includes "Kelly Wunstell" (interested party) alongside the videographer. Cannot assume ALSO PRESENT = videographer only.

7. **Attorney credential suffix variation**: ESQ. (universal), Paralegal (wunstell, halprin), no suffix (Jessica Quin in williams). BY: block cannot assume every name has a credential.

8. **Multi-email per firm**: halprin has firms with 2–4 email addresses. All others have 0 or 1. Data model must support a list, not a single string.

9. **Page position of appearances**: Ranges from page 3 (butler — short index) to page 5 (halprin, black_bp — longer index/exhibits). Not fixed.

10. **Court type affects cover + role labels**: State court (halprin) vs federal USDC (BP MDL) vs JAMS arbitration (olsen) produce different cover page formats and different role label vocabulary.

### Edge cases worth designing for

- **Party name wraps onto continuation line**: halprin "LONQUIST & COMPANY, LONQUIST FIELD / SERVICE:" — the party name itself wraps before the firm name. The `FOR THE DEFENDANT, [party]:` label is a multi-line construct, not a single-line header.
- **Same firm appears twice for different defendants**: Phelps Dunbar appears twice in halprin (National Casualty, Gemini) with different attorneys and different phone numbers. Renderer must not deduplicate on firm name.
- **Lugenbuhl Wheaton appears twice in halprin**: Once for Travelers (appearing) and once for RLI (NOT PRESENT). Same scenario.
- **Sparse page-2 with 19 blank lines**: The 2-page BP pattern produces a second appearances page where content ends at line 6 and lines 7–25 are blank. All 25 lines must still be emitted.
- **Email typo "nexsepruet.com"**: MB preserves verbatim; renderer must not validate or normalize email addresses.
- **Phone format inconsistency**: "504.581-7979" (wunstell/Liskow) vs "504.581.4892" (standard). Not normalized.
- **Double `@@` in email**: halprin page 6 has "asirianni@@windelsmarx.com" — double at-sign. Verbatim from source; renderer must not parse or validate.
- **Suite field absent**: Some firms have no suite line (butler's Nexsen Pruet: "205 King St / Suite 400" actually does have it, but Travelers in halprin has no suite). Not all firm blocks have the same sub-fields.

### Surprises vs the Halprin pattern

- The BP MDL cases (williams, butler, black_bp) use a **completely different block structure** than Halprin: the second page has only 5–6 lines of content followed by 19 blank lines. Halprin's page 2+ are dense with party blocks. These are not variations of the same layout — they are two distinct block patterns.
- The olsen **VIDEOGRAPHER: label** (no ALSO PRESENT header) was not anticipated. It's a single-line inline entry, not a block.
- Wunstell's **non-attorney in ALSO PRESENT** is the most fragile assumption to break. The ALSO PRESENT block cannot be typed as `videographer_name` only.
- **Appearances page number is not fixed**: ranges from 3 to 5 across this set. The renderer cannot hardcode a starting page number — it must receive it as a parameter (consistent with how stip and vid already work).
- The **"Via Zoom" annotation** is absent from all 2022 cases and present in 2025–2026 cases. This reflects pandemic-era vs. post-pandemic depo practice. It will appear in most new depos going forward.
